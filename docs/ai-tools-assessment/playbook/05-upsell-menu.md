# Upsell Menu (what to sell after the $999)

The $999 is just the door. The lifetime value of an assessment client lands at $3k, $5k, $10k or more. And you are not pitching cold: these people already paid you and already trust you, so a $5k Claude skills pack sells far easier here than to any Facebook-ad lead.

Six upsells, roughly small to large.

## 1. Process redesign / optimization

Fix a broken process without automating it yet. Map the current state, deliver the future-state blueprint: sixteen steps become seven. **Sold for $3k to $3.5k.**

> Example: an e-commerce seller spent ~10 hours a week monitoring Amazon ad spend and wanted it automated. Correct move: fix the process first, because throwing AI at a broken 16-step process just breaks faster. Deliverable was the redesigned future-state process. Automating it is a separate engagement.

## 2. Automation build

A straight Zapier, Make, or n8n flow. Best for cut-and-dry input to output, usually one to three steps, where a full Claude skill would be overkill. **Sold around $1,500.**

> Example: a wedding-venue business whose ops manager duplicated the same Asana project every new booking (~30 min/week, and growing with the business). One Zap, built once, scales with them.

## 3. Knowledge system

Package a business's repeated-question knowledge into something that answers for them. Often a custom GPT trained on their material.

> Example: a business broker got 400 to 500 emails per listing, all asking the same five questions (EBITDA, years in business, and so on). Built a custom GPT trained on the listing's marketing package; sent buyers the GPT link instead of his inbox. Pulled two levers at once: efficiency (zero repeat emails) and quality (buyer and seller both called it the best deal experience they had had).

## 4. Custom workflows

A proprietary, business-specific process turned into a Claude skill plus reference files. Train their team, hand it over, plug and play. Bake in **recurring revenue**: processes change, tools change, so charge monthly or quarterly to maintain and update the skills as the business evolves.

## 5. Retainer packaging

Wrap any of the above into a monthly deal, sized to the client. Examples: "$3k/month, I build you up to two knowledge systems a month." "$2k/month, up to three automated workflows a month." Get creative per client and their needs. Monthly sells more easily once you have shown a first win, so land one deliverable, then propose the retainer.

## 6. Full implementation

Any combination of the above bundled together. Two processes redesigned, a couple of Claude skills built, a Zapier flow. **An $8k package, for example.** This is the "all of the above" tier.

## The assessment-credit close (gets fence-sitters off the fence)

Offer to credit the $999 they already paid toward the implementation, so a $5k build becomes $4k. It works extremely well. The move that keeps you whole: **mark the upsell up by $1,000 first, then credit the $999.** They feel like they got a grand off; you make the same or a dollar more.

> **Before you use this, decide the policy and document it transparently.** The site's own follow-ups flag "decide whether a future implementation credit policy is useful and document it transparently before offering it." Do that first, then offer it consistently.

## Focus beats a big menu

Do not try to sell all six. Corey's advice: do the assessment (easy to fulfill), then pick **one** upsell lane to specialize in. Assessment then knowledge systems. Assessment then Claude skills. Assessment then the [AI Concierge retainer](06-ai-concierge-retainer.md), which is the recommended lane.
