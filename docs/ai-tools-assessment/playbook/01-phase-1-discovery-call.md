# Phase 1: Discovery Call (the question script)

**Goal:** pull out problems. Nothing else. You are probing for pain, not pitching, and not prescribing. Even when you already know the exact tool they need, bite your tongue. The first call is for probing only.

**Format:** Zoom or Google Meet, part of the 45-minute assessment. **Record it.**

**Duration:** about 30 to 45 minutes of questions.

## Before the call: setup

- [ ] Put an AI note-taker in the meeting (Fathom, Otter, or Fireflies). The transcript it produces is the input to Phase 2, so this is not optional.
- [ ] Get **explicit verbal consent** to record and to process the conversation with AI, on the recording, before you start asking questions. This is a hard gate. No consent, no recording, no assessment.
- [ ] Have the client's intake form (name, business, what they do) open so you are not burning call time on basics.

## The one rule

Do not prescribe on the first call. The moment you say "oh, you need Tool X," you stop learning and you start selling. Keep them talking. Write the tool ideas down silently and save them for the report.

## The core questions (ask these verbatim)

Ask them in roughly this order, and let each answer run. Silence is your friend.

1. **"Walk me through your day yesterday. What do you tend to do in a typical business day?"**
2. **"What are tasks in your business that you dread doing?"**
3. **"Where does your work pile up?"**
4. **"What are things that you've tried to automate in the past and failed?"**
5. **(The magic wand, the best one) "If you could wave a magic wand and delete any process in your business, what would that process be?"**

> Expect email. Very often the honest answer to the magic-wand question is "if I never had to answer an email again I would be a different person." That is highly solvable with AI, so probe it hard when it comes up.

## Follow-up probes (use to go one layer deeper on any pain they name)

These are scaffolding, not a script. Reach for one when an answer is thin.

- "How many hours a week does that eat?"
- "Who else touches that process? Where does the handoff break?"
- "What happens if that ball gets dropped?"
- "How are you doing it today, step by step?"
- "Have you tried to fix it before? What happened?"

## Capturing the pain

For every pain point, try to leave the call with three things, because Phase 2 and the report need them:

- The pain in one plain sentence.
- A rough **hours per week** it costs.
- Their **effective hourly rate** (or enough to estimate it), because the report's ROI math multiplies reclaimed hours by this number.

If you did not get the hourly rate live, mark it `unknown` and get it before the report goes out. Never guess it.

## Output of this phase

The recording and its transcript. That transcript goes straight into [Phase 2](02-phase-2-ai-research-prompt.md).
