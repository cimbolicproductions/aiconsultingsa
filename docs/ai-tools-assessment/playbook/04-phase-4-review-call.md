# Phase 4: Review Call (closing questions)

The report is done and emailed. This call turns the assessment into the next sale. Half the time, sometimes 60 percent, the client asks you to implement.

**Format:** Zoom, about 30 minutes, screen-sharing the report.

**Before the call:** email the report so they have time to look before you meet.

## The walkthrough

Share your screen and go recommendation by recommendation. Spend about half the call on the Recommended Solutions slide. The rhythm for each tool:

> "This is your pain point. This is the tool. It costs $10 a month, it takes you 30 minutes to set up, and it saves you 2 hours a week. Any questions? Great, next."

Boom, boom, boom. You are the doctor reading the prescription.

## The three closing questions (ask all three, verbatim)

At the end of the walkthrough:

1. **"Of these recommendations, which is the most urgent for you?"**
2. **"Do you want to do this yourself, or would you like my help implementing some of these solutions?"**
3. **"What's your timeline? Are these pain points killing you, or is this something you could live another 60 days with?"**

These three questions surface urgency, intent to buy, and timeline without you pitching. Half to sixty percent of clients answer question 2 with "can you just do it for me?"

## Tee up the upsell

When you reach the "What comes after quick wins" slide, walk through the Major Projects plainly. The client's own next question becomes "can you help us with that?" Now go to the [upsell menu](05-upsell-menu.md) or the [AI Concierge retainer](06-ai-concierge-retainer.md).

## Capture before you close

- [ ] Which recommendation they marked most urgent.
- [ ] Self-implement, or want help. (If help, which projects.)
- [ ] Timeline / urgency.
- [ ] The obvious next offer, and its price.

That is the entire assessment flow, beginning to end.
