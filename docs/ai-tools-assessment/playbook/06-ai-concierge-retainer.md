# AI Concierge Retainer (the recurring-revenue upsell)

The favorite upsell, and the one that produces a roughly $1,000/hour effective rate. Done-with-you consulting on a monthly retainer. Of the first six people Corey pitched it to, he closed five and hit $8k MRR in the first ten days, raising the price on each new yes.

## The offer in a nutshell

- **Two 45-minute calls per month.** The client shares their screen; you help them use Claude and build Claude skills. That is the core of every session.
- **Unlimited Voxer (voice) access between calls, 12-business-hour response.** Very high perceived value, near-zero real load. Of five clients, three had messaged once each in two and a half months. Do not fear it; price it as a premium perk.

At $1,500/month for two 45-minute calls, your effective rate is about $1,000/hour.

## Pricing ladder and scarcity

Corey quoted the first client $1,200/month, then $1,500, $1,800, and $2,000 as demand proved out. He caps the roster at six clients, and the scarcity is real: on every sales call he can honestly say he is taking one more client and has two other interested calls this week.

Landing-page tactic for the offer: tiered spots with real urgency.

```
4 spots at $800/month  →  then $1,200/month
4 spots at $1,200/month →  then $1,600/month
```

Services need built-in urgency or people stall, even when AI is already on their mind. Keep any scarcity legitimate.

## First call: onboarding only (set the foundation)

Most clients have never set Claude up properly. Call one builds nothing. You hand them an onboarding plugin and hand-hold them through it:

- Connect their tools.
- Create their context files.
- Create their global instructions.
- Show them what a scheduled task looks like.
- Show them what a skill does.

That is the entire first call. Every call after is ten times more effective because the foundation exists.

## Every call after: the AOA loop

Run **Audit, Optimize, Automate** on one workflow at a time:

1. **Audit**: "show me how you do it today." They walk through it.
2. **Optimize**: cut the fat. Thirteen steps become seven.
3. **Automate**: turn the optimized process into a Claude skill.

Rinse and repeat, workflow by workflow, for as long as they are a client.

## What makes it feel premium

**Pre-engagement onboarding form (a mini audit).** Before the first call, send a short Google Form with pointed questions. The one that matters most:

> **"90 days from now, what would make this engagement feel like a win?"**

Get a specific answer. Four of Corey's first five clients hit their stated 90-day win in about 60 days. Suggested starter questions for the form (adapt per client):

1. 90 days from now, what would make this engagement feel like a win? (Be specific.)
2. What is the single most time-consuming task in your week right now?
3. What have you tried with AI so far, and where did it stall?
4. Which tools and systems do you already use daily?
5. Who on your team, if anyone, will be in these sessions with you?

**A Notion hub, one page, updated after every call.** The whole engagement lives there, and it holds an **inventory of everything you have accomplished together.** Email it after every call. When they see "we did this, this, this, and this, this month" staring back at them, the $1,800 renews itself. It is also where you re-upsell: when they hit the 90-day win, remind them they said that would be success, note that you exceeded it, and propose what is next.

## Why it is the recommended lane

The assessment already uncovered the skills and workflows worth building. "If you want me to do it with you, I can be your in-house AI concierge and we build this together twice a month" is a far easier sentence than a cold pitch. Assessment first, concierge second.
